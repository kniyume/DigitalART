About ΕΜΣΤ

The National Museum of Contemporary Art (EMST) began its operation in 2000. Its permanent home is the former Fix factory, the reconstruction of which was completed in February 2014. From 2003 to 2008, EMST presented exhibitions and activities at the Athens Concert Hall, the Athens School of Fine Arts and in public spaces, while from 2008 to 2015, it was hosted in a part of the Athens Conservatory building. In May 2015, it moved into its permanent home, where from October 2016 to January 2019, it operated its temporary exhibition spaces. In February 2020, EMST opened to the public in full operation. EMST collects, preserves, conserves, documents, researches and exhibits works of contemporary Greek and international artistic production. Its continuous pursuit is the promotion of artistic education and public awareness regarding contemporary culture, in combination with the development of scientific research and specialization in museology, history and theory of contemporary art. An integral part of its founding objectives is the promotion of avant-garde and experimental artistic trends and the production of audiovisual works using new media. In the context of its social and educational role, EMST offers the public opportunities to interact with contemporary art through its exhibition activity, actions, programs and publications. The EMST collection is made up of works by Greek and foreign artists, in different media - painting, sculpture, video, installations, photography, engravings, industrial design, etc. - that address current and timeless issues. With a view to the broader development and promotion of contemporary art, EMST invests in artistic production and the achievement of synergies and collaborative relationships. EMST aspires to expand the domestic contemporary art community, while connecting it to the international scene. In a world in constant flux, EMST represents the exchange of ideas and opinions through contemporary art, enriching the everyday lives of the public and the experience of visitors.
